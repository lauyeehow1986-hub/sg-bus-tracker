# v10.2 — Live location on route panel

## What's new

When you open a route panel, the app now tracks your location via
`navigator.geolocation.watchPosition` and highlights the stop you're
currently nearest to.

States:

- **Near a stop on this route** — That stop gets a pulsing accent-
  coloured dot and a small "📍 You are here" marker. Banner at top
  of the list shows either "At stop #N" or (if you set a journey
  destination earlier) "N stops to go · ~M min".
- **Near your destination** — Banner changes to green "✓ You've
  reached your destination".
- **Not on this route but within 2km** — Subtle dashed banner
  "Not on this route · closest stop #N is Xm away". No highlight
  on any stop.
- **Far away (>2km from entire route)** — Silent. No banner,
  no markers.
- **GPS accuracy worse than 200m** — "GPS signal weak" notice,
  no highlight (avoids misleading marker placement).
- **Permission denied or geolocation unavailable** — Silent.
  Feature simply doesn't activate; rest of the app works normally.

## Design notes

- Only subscribes to geolocation while the route panel is open.
  Unsubscribes on close (zero battery cost when not in use).
- `enableHighAccuracy: false` — stop-level precision is enough, and
  high-accuracy GPS is significantly more battery-hungry.
- DOM updates throttled to one per 3 seconds so rapid GPS ticks
  don't spam the main thread.
- Auto-scrolls to the current stop exactly once when first detected.
  Won't keep yanking your view as you move.
- Uses direct class/attribute manipulation instead of re-rendering
  the route list, so it doesn't fight the crowd-fetch re-render
  guards introduced in v9.1.
- `setActiveRoute` detects when the stop list genuinely changed
  (direction switch) vs. when the same stops are re-rendered (crowd
  refresh) — only the first case resets the scrolling behaviour.

## Honest limitations

- **Cannot tell whether you're on the bus, walking, or in a car.**
  Anything that puts you within 100m of a stop triggers the marker.
- **GPS fails in tunnels and deep between tall buildings.** The
  last-known marker will stick until signal returns. This is a
  hardware limitation, not something the app can fix.
- **Bus direction is your responsibility.** If a route passes
  your current location in both directions, switch tabs to see
  the marker for the one you're actually travelling on.
- **Time-to-destination estimate is rough** — 2 min per stop
  flat. Real urban buses vary with traffic, lights, and dwell
  time at busy stops. Treat it as a rough guide, not a
  countdown timer.
