# v10.10 — Nearby stops show all within 600m

## What was wrong

The "Nearby stops" feature capped the result list at 8 stops, even
though the 600m radius can legitimately contain 15-25 stops in dense
areas like Bedok, Tampines, or the CBD. Your screenshot showed "8
FOUND" — that was 8 of probably 12-15 stops actually within walking
range.

## Why it was 8

Looking at the code, no comment or rationale. Most likely the original
author picked 8 as "looks fine on a phone screen." Same kind of silent
truncation as the search-results cap from v10.9 (which I fixed there
but missed here).

## The fix

Removed the `.slice(0, 8)` from the filter pipeline. Now the feature
shows all stops within 600m, sorted by distance.

## Why no high cap like search has

Search can match thousands of stops on common queries ("rd", "ave"),
so a safety ceiling makes sense there. But "nearby stops within 600m"
is bounded by geography. Even in the densest parts of Singapore, the
maximum is around 25-30 stops. There's no pathological case where
removing the cap could hurt.

## What you'll see

In your Bedok area screenshot scenario: probably 12-15 nearby stops
instead of 8. The badge "N FOUND" will reflect the actual count.

In sparser areas (suburban estates, industrial parks): you'll see
maybe 4-6 stops, same as before — the 600m radius was always the
real bound for those cases.
