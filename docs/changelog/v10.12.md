# v10.12 — Per-stop progress announcements + hidden-tab robustness

## What's new

When you arm the alighting alarm, the app now announces each stop the
bus passes along the way to your destination:

  "Just passed Bedok Sports Cplx. 8 stops to go."
  "Just passed The Tanamera Condo. 7 stops to go."
  "Just passed Bedok Camp 2. 6 stops to go."
  ...
  "Just passed Opp Blk 84. 1 stop to go."
  [chime + vibration] "Approaching Blk 55"
  [stronger chime + vibration] "Arriving at Blk 55. Press the bell now."

Per-stop announcements are **voice only** — no chime, no vibration.
Just the destination alerts (250m and 80m) keep their full
chime+vibration treatment.

## What you don't hear

- **Stops you passed before arming.** The alarm finds the nearest stop
  on the first GPS fix after arming, marks it and all earlier stops as
  silently visited. So if you arm at stop #58, announcements start from
  #59 onward.
- **The destination as "just passed."** Stop #67 is not announced
  with "just passed" — it gets the proper Arriving alert instead.
- **Stops you've already heard about.** Each stop announces once.
  Re-entering its 80m radius (e.g., bus loops) won't repeat.

## Hidden-tab robustness improvements

You asked whether this works when you switch to another Chrome tab.
Honest answer: mostly yes, with caveats — but I added several
improvements to make the hidden-tab experience as good as possible:

**1. Skipped-threshold detection.** In a hidden tab, position updates
slow to once every 30-60 seconds. The bus might pass through both
the 250m and 80m thresholds between two fixes. Previously this
fired only the 80m alert. Now it fires both (approaching first, then
arriving 1.5 seconds later) so you hear them in correct order.

**2. Persistent audio context.** The WebAudio context used for the
chimes is now created once on arm (during the user gesture) and kept
alive until disarm. Previously each chime created a fresh context,
which is more likely to fail in a hidden tab. The persistent context
auto-resumes after Android tab-hidden suspension.

**3. Speech queueing.** Per-stop announcements use queued speech (not
barge-in). If updates batch together in a hidden tab, you'll hear
them in sequence rather than only the latest.

**4. Arriving alert clears the queue.** When the Arriving alert fires,
it cancels any queued per-stop announcements — the user is arriving
NOW and shouldn't hear "Just passed N stops to go" first.

**5. Auto-disarm bumped 5s → 8s** to give the final speech time to
finish before everything tears down.

## What still doesn't work

**Phone locked.** Geolocation stops on most Android browsers when the
device is locked. Alarm pauses entirely until unlocked.

**Phone in deep doze (extended idle).** Same as locked — Android
freezes JavaScript execution.

**Switching to a different app entirely.** Chrome may pause the PWA
process. Even tab-hidden caveats are best-case.

These are PWA architecture limits, not bugs. If you need pocket-the-
phone reliability, SG BusLeh is the right tool for that case.

## Speech abbreviation expansion

Inherited from v10.11. ~25 common abbreviations expanded so TTS reads
naturally: Blk → Block, Stn → Station, Opp → Opposite, Cplx → Complex,
Sec Sch → Secondary School, Int → Interchange, etc.

## How it sounds in practice

Imagine bus 14 from Bef Sennett Rd to Blk 55 New Upp Changi Rd
(13 stops):

  Bus departs Bef Sennett Rd (#54, your boarding stop — silent)
  Passes Bef Bedok N Rd 4 (#55) → "Just passed Before Bedok North Road 4. 12 stops to go."
  Passes Aft Bedok N Rd 4 (#56) → "Just passed After Bedok North Road 4. 11 stops to go."
  ... (10 more announcements)
  Passes Opp Blk 32 (#65) → "Just passed Opposite Block 32. 2 stops to go."
  Passes Blk 55 (#66) → "Just passed Block 55. 1 stop to go."
  Bus reaches 250m from Blk 55 → vibrate + chime + "Approaching Blk 55"
  Bus reaches 80m from Blk 55 → stronger vibrate + double chime + "Arriving at Blk 55. Press the bell now."
  Auto-disarm 8s later.
