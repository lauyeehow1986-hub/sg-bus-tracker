# v10.3 — Auto-direction-switch on route panel

## What's new

When you open a route panel for a bus and start moving, the app figures
out which direction you're actually travelling and silently switches to
that tab. A brief "Switched to → Bedok Int (auto)" toast appears with an
"Undo" button so you can revert if the guess was wrong.

## Confidence model

The switch only fires when ALL of these are true:

1. The route has exactly 2 directions (single-direction loops skip auto-switch)
2. At least 30 seconds since the first GPS fix in this panel session
3. User has moved ≥50m between the two readings (rules out GPS jitter
   while stationary)
4. Both readings are within 100m of *some* stop on the route (you're
   actually on the route, not just somewhere in town)
5. Sequence advancement on the recommended direction is ≥2 stops AND
   greater than advancement on the other direction (clear winner)
6. User hasn't manually tapped a tab in this session
7. We haven't already auto-switched in this session (one-shot)

If any guard fails, nothing happens. The current direction stays.

## Behaviour after manual override

The moment you tap a direction tab manually, auto-switch turns off
permanently for the current panel session. Re-opening the panel resets
the state.

The "Undo" button on the auto-switch toast also disables it for the
session — if our guess was wrong, we won't pester you again.

## What this fixes

Previously, opening a route panel always defaulted to direction 1.
If you boarded the bus going the other way, you had to tap the other
tab to see the right stops. Now the app figures it out from your
movement.

## What this doesn't try to do

- **Auto-switch when you're stationary.** If you're checking the route
  in advance from home, no movement = no switch. The original direction
  stays.
- **Switch to a different bus route.** The app only switches direction
  within the route you opened. It never assumes you've changed buses.
- **Detect mid-trip turn-arounds.** Buses don't usually turn around
  mid-route, and the one-shot guard prevents flip-flopping.

## Honest limitations

- GPS error of 30-100m is normal. The 50m movement threshold + 30s
  interval reduces false positives but doesn't eliminate them.
- Underground / tunnel sections produce no GPS. If you board there,
  the first useful fix arrives once you surface, and the algorithm
  starts from that point.
- Same-name overlapping stops (some bus 14 stops are within 30m on
  both directions of Upp East Coast Rd) might cause both directions
  to "advance" together briefly. The "advancement ≥ 2 AND clearly
  greater" guard usually waits until you've passed those overlap
  zones before deciding.
