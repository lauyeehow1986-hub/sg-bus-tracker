# v10.8 — Search no longer truncates results for long roads

## The bug

Searching "new upp changi rd" returned only 7 stops, even though the
road has ~30 stops (e.g. Blk 55 at code 84069 wasn't in the results).

## Why it happened

`findStops` in `stops.js` had two limits compounding:

1. The function defaulted to `limit = 7`
2. Worse, the search loop bailed early at `limit * 3` (21) candidates
   to "save time"

For long roads with 30+ stops, the loop would fill up its 21
candidates with the first 21 stops it encountered (sorted by stop
code), then break. The final `slice(0, 7)` returned only the lowest-
numbered stop codes — everything beyond was silently dropped.

For "new upp changi rd": the early stops (84011, 84019, 84021, 84029,
84031, 84039, 84041) won. Stops beyond (84051, 84061, 84069, etc.)
never even got considered.

## The fix

Two changes in `findStops`:

1. **Removed the early bail.** The loop now scans all ~5500 stops
   regardless. Linear scan over a small array — ~0.5ms even on a
   weak phone. Performance cost is negligible.
2. **Default limit raised from 7 to 30.** The result list is
   already scrollable in the UI; showing 30 candidates instead of
   7 is fine and gives complete results for long-road searches.

Both call sites (main search bar and planner destination picker)
now use the default.

## What you'll see

Searching "new upp changi rd" now shows all stops on the road in
order, not just the first 7. The same applies to other long roads:
Bedok North Rd, Tampines Ave 5, etc.

For short queries (specific stop names like "Blk 55"), behaviour is
unchanged — they already returned <7 results.
